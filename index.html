Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet (B1+)

Created by Panagiotis Domvros, English and Drama Teacher

In the beautiful city of Verona, Italy, two important families, the Montagues and the Capulets, were enemies. Their long and bitter feud often caused violent fights in the streets. The Prince of Verona had warned them to stop, or they would be punished by death.

Young Romeo, a Montague, was not interested in the fighting. He was sad because the woman he loved, Rosaline, did not love him back. He often walked alone through the city, feeling heartbroken. His cousin, Benvolio, tried to cheer him up.

Meanwhile, young Juliet, a Capulet who was almost fourteen, was talking with her mother. Lady Capulet told Juliet that a noble man, Count Paris, wanted to marry her. Juliet was not sure what to think. She had never thought about marriage before.

That night, the Capulets were having a big party. Romeo’s friends, Benvolio and Mercutio, convinced him to go. They would wear masks so no one would recognise them as Montagues. Mercutio joked that Romeo might find a new woman to love at the party.

At the party, Romeo forgot all about Rosaline. He saw Juliet and immediately fell in love. He thought she was the most beautiful girl he had ever seen. He went to speak to her, and when they touched hands, they both felt a special connection.

After the party, Romeo secretly went to the Capulet’s garden. He saw Juliet on her balcony. She was speaking to the stars, asking why the boy she liked had to be a Montague. Romeo stepped out from the shadows, and they confessed their love for each other.

The next day, Romeo went to see Friar Laurence, a kind priest. Romeo told him about his love for Juliet and asked the Friar to marry them in secret. The Friar was surprised, but he agreed. He hoped their marriage could end the terrible feud between their families.

With the help of Juliet’s Nurse, Romeo and Juliet met at Friar Laurence’s church. There, in a secret ceremony with no family present, they were married. For a moment, they were filled with joy and hope for their future together.

But their happiness did not last long. Later that day, Juliet’s cousin, Tybalt, saw Romeo in the street. Tybalt was still angry that Romeo had come to the Capulet party. He challenged Romeo to a fight, but Romeo refused. Mercutio, angry at Tybalt’s insults, drew his sword to fight instead.

Romeo tried to stop the fight, but it was too late. Tybalt killed Mercutio. Filled with grief and anger for his dead friend, Romeo forgot his promise to love the Capulets. He fought Tybalt and killed him.

For killing Tybalt, the Prince of Verona banished Romeo from the city. He had to leave Verona immediately and could never return. The news was terrible for both Romeo and Juliet. Their new marriage now seemed impossible.

To make things worse, Juliet’s father decided she must marry Count Paris in three days. He thought it would help her with her sadness over Tybalt’s death. When Juliet refused, her father became very angry and threatened to disown her.

Desperate, Juliet went to Friar Laurence for help. He gave her a special potion. He told her it would make her sleep so deeply that everyone would think she was dead. The family would put her in the family tomb. The Friar promised to send a message to Romeo, who would return to rescue her when she woke up.

But the Friar’s message never reached Romeo. Instead, Romeo’s servant arrived in Mantua and told him that Juliet was dead. Heartbroken, Romeo bought a strong poison from an apothecary and decided to return to Verona to die beside his wife.

Romeo went to the Capulet family tomb. He found Juliet lying there, looking as beautiful as ever. He did not know she was only sleeping. He gave her one last kiss, drank the poison, and fell dead beside her.

Moments later, Juliet woke up and saw Romeo dead. Friar Laurence arrived too late to stop the tragedy. Full of sorrow, Juliet took Romeo’s dagger and killed herself. When the families arrived and saw their dead children, their long feud finally ended, but it was a peace bought with the greatest price.

Scenes and Dialogues

Picture 1: The Feud

Prince: Stop your fighting! The next to break the peace will pay with their life.

Lord Capulet: Our hate for the Montagues is too strong.

Lord Montague: We will never forgive the Capulets.

Picture 2: Sad Romeo

Benvolio: Cousin, why do you look so sad?

Romeo: The woman I love, Rosaline, does not love me.

Benvolio: You must forget her. Let's find someone new.

Picture 3: Juliet and Paris

Lady Capulet: Juliet, the noble Count Paris wants to marry you.

Juliet: Marry? I have not thought about it, Mother.

Lady Capulet: You will meet him tonight at our party.

Picture 4: The Masked Party

Mercutio: Come on, Romeo! Let's go to the party.

Romeo: We are Montagues. It is too dangerous.

Benvolio: We will wear masks! No one will know who we are.

Picture 5: Love at First Sight

Romeo: Who is that girl? She is the most beautiful I have ever seen.

Juliet: The man with the mask... I must know his name.

Romeo: If I touch your hand, I will be blessed.

Picture 6: The Balcony Scene

Juliet: Oh, Romeo. Why must you be a Montague?

Romeo: (from the shadows) I will change my name for you, my love.

Juliet: Romeo! You heard me? This is so dangerous!

Picture 7: The Friar's Plan

Romeo: Friar, I have forgotten Rosaline. I am in love with Juliet!

Friar Laurence: A Capulet? This is very sudden.

Romeo: Will you marry us in secret? Please?

Friar Laurence: I will. Perhaps your love can end your families' hate.

Picture 8: The Secret Wedding

Friar Laurence: Come, we must be quick.

Romeo: I am so happy, I can't speak.

Juliet: My love for you is just as great.

Picture 9: The Challenge

Tybalt: Romeo, you are a villain! Draw your sword!

Romeo: Tybalt, I cannot fight you. I have a reason to love you.

Mercutio: He will not fight? Then I will!

Picture 10: The Death of Mercutio

Mercutio: I... I am hurt. A curse on both your houses!

Romeo: My friend is dead!

Romeo: Now, Tybalt, you will pay for this!

Picture 11: The Banishment

Prince: Romeo killed Tybalt. For this, he is banished from Verona!

Romeo: Banished? This is worse than death!

Juliet: My husband is banished and my cousin is dead. What will I do?

Picture 12: The Forced Marriage

Lord Capulet: You are sad about Tybalt. You will marry Paris in three days!

Juliet: I will not! I cannot marry him!

Lord Capulet: You will obey me or I will throw you into the street!

Picture 13: The Potion

Juliet: I will kill myself before I marry Paris!

Friar Laurence: Wait! I have a plan. Drink this potion.

Friar Laurence: It will make you sleep like you are dead. Romeo will come for you.

Picture 14: The Wrong News

Romeo: What is the news from Verona?

Balthasar: My lord... I saw them bury Juliet in her family's tomb.

Romeo: Juliet is dead? Then I will go to her tonight!

Picture 15: Romeo's Death

Romeo: (in the tomb) Oh, my love. Still so beautiful.

Romeo: I will stay with you forever. Here's to my love!

Romeo: (drinks poison) And so, with a kiss, I die.

Picture 16: Juliet's Death

Juliet: (wakes up) My Romeo? Dead?

Friar Laurence: Come away, Juliet! The guards are coming!

Juliet: No, I will stay. Oh, a happy dagger! This is my sheath. (kills herself)
